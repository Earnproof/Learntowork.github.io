Test Your Risk Management Knowledge

15 questions covering the 1% rule, stop loss, position sizing, risk/reward ratio, leverage, and trading psychology.

Question 1 of 15 Score: 0
Question 1 of 15
What does the "1% rule" mean in trading?
Correct! The 1% rule means you should never risk more than 1% of your total trading account on any single trade. On a $1,000 account, your maximum loss per trade is $10.
Question 2 of 15
If you have a $2,000 trading account and follow the 1% rule, what is your maximum loss per trade?
Correct! $2,000 × 1% = $20 maximum risk per trade. Simple math — but most beginners never calculate this before entering a trade.
Question 3 of 15
What is a stop loss in trading?
Correct! A stop loss automatically closes your trade at a predetermined price level, preventing your loss from growing larger. Trading without a stop loss is one of the most dangerous habits in trading.
Question 4 of 15
What is the minimum risk/reward ratio recommended for beginner traders?
Correct! A minimum of 1:2 risk/reward is recommended for beginners — for every $1 you risk, aim to make $2. This means you can be wrong 60% of the time and still be profitable.
Question 5 of 15
With a 1:3 risk/reward ratio, what is the minimum win rate needed to be consistently profitable?
Correct! With a 1:3 risk/reward ratio, you only need to win 25% of trades to break even, and around 35% to be consistently profitable. You can lose 65% of your trades and still make money.
Question 6 of 15
What is "revenge trading"?
Correct! Revenge trading is one of the most dangerous patterns — taking impulsive, oversized trades after a loss trying to quickly win back money. It almost always results in even bigger losses. After any loss, take a 30-minute break.
Question 7 of 15
What is position sizing?
Correct! Position sizing connects your account size, risk percentage, and stop loss distance into one precise calculation. It tells you exactly how many units, shares, or lots to buy so you never risk more than planned.
Question 8 of 15
What is the WORST thing to do when price is approaching your stop loss?
Correct! Moving your stop loss further away when price approaches it is one of the most dangerous habits. Your stop was placed for a reason. Moving it means risking more than planned — and hope is not a strategy. Let your stop loss do its job.
Question 9 of 15
What does leverage do in trading?
Correct! Leverage amplifies both profits AND losses equally. With 100:1 leverage, a 1% move against you can wipe your entire account. Beginners should use maximum 1:10 leverage — far less than most brokers offer.
Question 10 of 15
What is a recommended maximum daily loss limit?
Correct! The 3% daily stop rule means: if you lose 3% of your account in one day, stop trading — no exceptions. This prevents one bad day from becoming a catastrophic week. Professional traders follow this religiously.
Question 11 of 15
What does ATR stand for in trading?
Correct! ATR stands for Average True Range. It measures how much an asset normally moves in a given period. Setting your stop loss at 1.5x or 2x ATR gives the trade enough room to breathe without being too wide.
Question 12 of 15
Which type of stop loss is considered most professional?
Correct! Structure-based stop loss placement — just below key support (for longs) or above key resistance (for shorts) — is the most professional approach. It is based on actual market structure, not an arbitrary number.
Question 13 of 15
What is "correlation risk" in portfolio management?
Correct! Correlation risk means trading multiple assets that move in the same direction — like EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Even if each trade risks 1%, they are really one big bet on USD weakening. Your true exposure is much higher than it appears.
Question 14 of 15
Which emotion causes traders to exit winning trades too early?
Correct! Fear makes traders exit winning trades too early (cutting profits short), hesitate on valid setups, and freeze when they should execute. Fear comes from risking too much — which is another reason the 1% rule also solves a psychology problem.
Question 15 of 15
Before trading real money, what is the MOST important thing to do?
Correct! Practice on a demo account for a minimum of 3-6 months, with at least 50-100 tracked trades. If you cannot make consistent profits on demo, real money will not fix that — it will only make losses hurt more. Patience here is the most profitable decision you can make.
