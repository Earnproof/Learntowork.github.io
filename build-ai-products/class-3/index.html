2 What Exactly Is a Custom GPT?

Think of the regular ChatGPT as a generalist — it knows everything but specializes in nothing. A Custom GPT is a specialist version you create, trained on your instructions, your knowledge, and your rules.

Regular ChatGPTYour Custom GPT
Knows everything, specializes in nothingTrained on your specific topic
Different personality every conversationAlways uses your defined tone and style
No memory of your business or contextHas all your knowledge files loaded
Anyone can use it for anythingFocused on exactly one job
You have to re-explain every timeAlready knows everything you told it — permanently
Cannot be shared as a productCan be shared, published, or sold

The key difference: a Custom GPT remembers its instructions forever — across every conversation, with every user. You set it up once, and it behaves consistently every single time. That is what makes it a real product, not just a prompt.

4 Build Your Custom GPT — Step by Step

Open ChatGPT right now and follow along. By the time you finish reading this section, your Custom GPT will be ready. Do not just read — build.

The GPT Builder interface — every section labeled. Follow along with the steps below.

1

Open the GPT Builder

Go to chatgpt.com → click your profile icon (top right) → select "My GPTs" → click "Create a GPT" (green button, top right).

You will see two tabs: Create and Configure. The Create tab uses a conversation to help you build. The Configure tab gives you manual control. We will use the Configure tab — it gives you the most control and teaches you how it really works.

💡 Tip: If you do not see "Create a GPT," you may be on the free plan. Log in and go directly to: chat.openai.com/gpts/editor
2

Name Your GPT and Add a Description

Click the Configure tab. At the top you will see the Name field and Description field.

Name: Give it a clear, specific name. Not "My GPT" — something like "Freelance Pricing Guide" or "Study Helper for Biology" or "Layla's Customer Service Bot."

Description: One or two sentences explaining what it does. This is what users see before they start chatting. Example: "Your personal AI guide for setting freelance rates, writing proposals, and handling client negotiations."

💡 Tip: The name and description affect how people find your GPT in the store — use words your target user would actually search for.
3

Write the Instructions — This Is the Most Important Step

The Instructions box is the brain of your Custom GPT. This is where you tell it who it is, what it does, how it speaks, and what it should never do. Think of it as writing a job description for a very intelligent employee.

Here is a proven instructions template you can copy and customize:

📋 Instructions Template — Copy & Customize You are [NAME], a [ROLE] who helps [TARGET AUDIENCE] with [SPECIFIC TOPIC]. Your personality is [TONE: e.g. friendly and encouraging / professional and direct / warm but concise]. You always [POSITIVE BEHAVIOR: e.g. give specific examples / break things into steps / ask clarifying questions before answering]. You NEVER [RESTRICTION: e.g. give medical advice / make up prices / answer questions outside your topic]. When someone asks you [COMMON QUESTION TYPE], you always [HOW TO RESPOND]. Start every conversation by [OPENING BEHAVIOR: e.g. asking what they need help with today / introducing yourself briefly / asking one question to understand their situation]. Always end responses with [CLOSING BEHAVIOR: e.g. one follow-up question / a next step they can take / an offer to go deeper on any point].

Example — filled in for a Freelance Pricing GPT:

✅ Real Example Instructions You are PriceRight, a freelance business coach who helps creative freelancers — writers, designers, video editors, and social media managers — confidently set their rates and handle pricing conversations with clients. Your personality is direct, practical, and encouraging. You give specific numbers and real examples, never vague advice. You always break your answers into 2-3 clear steps and ask one follow-up question at the end to help the person go deeper. You NEVER give legal or tax advice. You NEVER make up market rates — if you are unsure about a specific niche rate, you say so and suggest how to research it. When someone asks how much to charge, you first ask: their skill level, location, and what the client's budget seems to be. Then give a specific range with reasoning. Start every conversation by saying: "Hi! I'm PriceRight — your freelance pricing coach. What's the pricing challenge you're dealing with today?" Always end with one actionable next step the person can take in the next 24 hours.
💡 Tip: Longer, more specific instructions = better, more consistent behavior. Most beginners write 3 lines. Write at least 150–200 words for a Custom GPT that actually works reliably.
4

Add Conversation Starters

Scroll down to "Conversation starters" — these are button prompts that appear at the top of every chat. They help users know what to ask and make the first interaction easy.

Add 3–4 starters that represent your GPT's most common and most valuable use cases. Examples for PriceRight:

💬 Example Conversation Starters "How much should I charge for a 1000-word blog post?" "A client thinks my rates are too high — how do I respond?" "I'm just starting out — what's a fair rate to begin with?" "Help me write a pricing section for my proposal"
💡 Tip: Make starters sound like real questions your users would actually type — not formal or robotic. Natural language gets more clicks.
5

Upload Knowledge Files (Optional but Powerful)

Under "Knowledge" you can upload files — PDFs, Word docs, text files, spreadsheets. Your GPT will read these and use them to answer questions. This is what makes it a true expert.

What to upload:

→ Your own rate card or pricing guide (PDF)
→ A document with common client objections and how to handle them
→ A list of your services with descriptions
→ Any reference material relevant to your GPT's topic

If you do not have files ready yet, skip this step — your GPT will still work great on instructions alone. You can add files later.

💡 Tip: Files must be under 512MB each. Plain text and PDF work best. The more specific and relevant the file, the better your GPT's answers will be.
6

Set Capabilities and Save

Under "Capabilities" you will see toggle switches. Turn on what your GPT needs:

→ Web Search — turn ON if your GPT needs current information (news, prices, recent events)
→ DALL·E Image Generation — turn ON if your GPT should create images
→ Code Interpreter — turn ON if your GPT should analyze data or write code

For most beginner GPTs, leave all three OFF. It keeps things fast and focused.

When ready, click Save (top right) → choose "Only me" (private) or "Anyone with a link" (shareable). Click Confirm.

🎉 Your Custom GPT is live! You will get a link you can share with anyone. Click "View GPT" to test it yourself — try one of your conversation starters and see how it responds.

5 Test It, Break It, Make It Better

Building the first version is step one. The real skill is testing it like a user — not like the person who built it — and improving it until it is something you would actually share with confidence.

Test questions to ask your GPT right now:

→ Ask something completely off-topic — does it stay focused or wander?
→ Ask something vague — does it ask for clarification or guess?
→ Ask your most important question — is the answer accurate, specific, and helpful?
→ Ask the same question in three different ways — are the answers consistent?

When you find something that does not work — go back to Edit GPT → Configure → Instructions and add a rule. For example: "If the user asks about [X], always respond by [Y]." Then test again.

⚠️ The most common mistake: Building a Custom GPT and never testing it from a real user's perspective. Always test with questions you did NOT write the instructions for. If it fails those tests, your real users will find the same problems.

Great Custom GPTs are not built — they are iterated. The first version is a draft. Test → improve → test again. After 3–5 rounds of improvements, you will have something genuinely useful.
