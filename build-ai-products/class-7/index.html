🎓 Series 2 — Build AI Chatbots · Class 7

Build an AI Appointment Booking Chatbot — Let Customers Book 24/7

⏱ 30 min read 🛠 Live build-along 📝 Quiz at the end 🆓 Free tools only

1 Your Phone Buzzes at 11 PM — "Are You Free Tomorrow?"

It's late. You're asleep, or out with family, or just done for the day. A potential customer messages: "Hi, do you have any slots free this week?" You don't see it until the morning. By then, they've already booked with someone else who replied faster.

Without a booking chatbot: missed messages, missed bookings. With one: customers book themselves, any time, day or night.

In Class 6, you built a chatbot that answers questions and handles complaints. Today, you take it one step further — you build a chatbot that lets customers actually book a time slot with you, see your real availability, and get confirmed — without a single message back and forth.

This single feature can be the difference between a business that's "always busy" and one that's actually getting bookings. Studies on appointment automation consistently show businesses that offer 24/7 self-booking convert significantly more enquiries into confirmed appointments — simply because customers book the moment they're interested, instead of waiting for a reply and losing momentum.

⚡ What you need: Your Chatbase chatbot from Class 6 (or a new one). A free Calendly account. 30 minutes. That is all.

2 Customer Service Chatbot vs Booking Chatbot — What Changes?

Your Class 6 chatbot can talk about your services and hours. A booking chatbot goes further — it shows real, live availability and lets the customer pick a slot.

Customer service chatbot = tells you about availability. Booking chatbot = lets you grab it, instantly.

FeatureCustomer Service Chatbot (Class 6)Booking Chatbot (Class 7)
Answers FAQs about services✅ Yes✅ Yes
Tells customer your working hours✅ Yes — as text✅ Yes — as text
Shows real, live available time slots❌ No✅ Yes — synced with your calendar
Lets customer pick and confirm a slot❌ No — customer must message you✅ Yes — booked instantly
Sends confirmation automatically❌ No✅ Yes — email confirmation to both sides
Prevents double-booking❌ Not possible✅ Yes — calendar blocks the slot
Works while you sleepPartially — answers questions only✅ Fully — books real appointments
🍋 Think of it like this

A customer service chatbot is like a receptionist who can describe the salon's services but has to say "let me check and call you back" for booking. A booking chatbot is that same receptionist — but with the actual appointment book open in front of them, writing the customer in right now.

3 Booking Scenarios Your Chatbot Will Handle

Before building, picture how a real conversation should flow. Here are the situations your booking chatbot needs to handle smoothly:

📅 Scenario 1

New Booking Request

Customer says: "I'd like to book a haircut for Friday." → Chatbot shares the booking link, customer picks a free slot and confirms.

🔍 Scenario 2

Checking Availability

Customer asks: "Are you free this weekend?" → Chatbot explains how to view live availability via the booking link — no guessing, no back-and-forth.

🔁 Scenario 3

Reschedule / Cancel

Customer says: "I need to change my appointment." → Chatbot points them to the reschedule/cancel link from their confirmation email.

🌙 Scenario 4

After-Hours Booking

Customer books at 1 AM while you're asleep → Calendar blocks the slot instantly, confirmation email sent, you see it in the morning — already done.

4 Set Up Your Calendar — The Foundation of the Booking System

Before touching Chatbase again, set up the calendar that will actually hold your appointments. We'll use Calendly — free, simple, and works with Google Calendar, Outlook, and Apple Calendar.

Go to calendly.com and create a free account. Once inside, fill in the details below — write them down first so you know exactly what to enter:

📝 Booking Setup Template — Fill This In FirstBUSINESS / SERVICE INFORMATION Business Name: [Your Business Name] Service being booked: [e.g. Haircut & Styling / Consultation Call / Tutoring Session] Appointment duration: [e.g. 30 minutes / 1 hour] Location: [e.g. In-person at [address] / Online via Zoom / Phone call] AVAILABILITY Days available: [e.g. Monday–Saturday] Hours available: [e.g. 10 AM – 7 PM] Buffer time between appointments: [e.g. 15 minutes — so you have breathing room] Minimum notice required: [e.g. at least 2 hours before — stops last-second bookings] BOOKING RULES Can customers cancel/reschedule themselves? [Yes/No] How far in advance can someone book? [e.g. up to 30 days ahead] Maximum bookings per day: [e.g. 8 appointments] CONFIRMATION DETAILS What should the confirmation email say? [e.g. "Thanks for booking! Please arrive 5 minutes early. Address: ..."] Reminder before appointment: [e.g. 1 hour before, via email]
1

Create Your Event Type in Calendly

Inside Calendly, click "Create" → "Event Type" → "One-on-One". Name it using your service (e.g. "Haircut Appointment" or "Free Consultation Call"). Set the duration using the info from your template above.

💡 Tip: Keep the event name short and clear — this is what the customer sees when booking, so "Haircut — 30 min" is better than "Booking Form for Services."
2

Set Your Real Availability

In the event type settings, go to "Availability". Enter the days and hours you actually want to take appointments. Add a buffer time (15 minutes is common) so back-to-back bookings don't leave you with zero breathing room.

Also set "Minimum scheduling notice" — e.g. 2 hours — so someone can't book an appointment for 5 minutes from now.

💡 Connect your calendar: Link your Google/Outlook/Apple calendar in Calendly settings. This way, if you already have something booked elsewhere, Calendly automatically hides that time slot — no double bookings.
3

Customize Your Confirmation Message

Go to "Notifications and workflows". Edit the confirmation email customers receive after booking — add your address, what to bring, or any special instructions from your template.

Turn on a reminder email (1 hour or 1 day before) — this alone reduces no-shows significantly.

4

Copy Your Booking Link

On your event type, click "Copy Link". It will look like calendly.com/yourname/haircut-30min. This link is the key piece — your chatbot is going to hand this exact link to customers who want to book.

💡 Test it yourself first: Open the link in a new tab and book a test appointment. Make sure the available times look correct and the confirmation email arrives properly before connecting it to your chatbot.

⚠️ Get your calendar right before moving on. If your availability or buffer times are wrong here, your chatbot will confidently send customers a link to a calendar that's wrong — and they'll book the wrong slots. Test the link yourself first.

5 Teach Your Chatbot to Handle Bookings

Now go back to your Chatbase chatbot from Class 6. You're going to add booking-specific training content and update the instructions so the chatbot knows exactly when and how to offer your booking link.

First, add this to your training content (Chatbase → your chatbot → add a new text source, or edit your existing one):

📝 Booking Training Content — Add to ChatbaseAPPOINTMENT BOOKING We accept appointment bookings online — available 24/7. Service offered: [Service name from your template] Duration: [e.g. 30 minutes] Location: [In-person address / Online link / Phone] Booking link: [PASTE YOUR CALENDLY LINK HERE] Availability: [e.g. Monday–Saturday, 10 AM – 7 PM] Bookings must be made at least [X hours] in advance. To book: Click the booking link, choose an available time slot, enter your name and email, and you'll receive an instant confirmation email with all the details. To reschedule or cancel: Use the link in your confirmation email — you can change your appointment yourself, no need to contact us directly. If a customer asks "are you free on [a date]" — do not guess. Always direct them to the booking link to see live, accurate availability.

Next, add this to your Instructions / System Prompt (Settings → AI → Instructions) — add it underneath your existing Class 6 instructions, don't replace them:

📋 Booking Instructions — Add to Your System PromptBOOKING REQUESTS: When a customer wants to book an appointment, or asks about availability: 1. Confirm what service they want (if you offer more than one) 2. Share the booking link and say: "You can see all available times and book instantly here: [LINK]" 3. Mention they'll get an instant email confirmation 4. If they ask "do you have anything on [date]?" — say: "The booking page shows live availability — you'll see exactly what's open on [date] when you click through: [LINK]" DO NOT: - Do not tell a customer a specific time is available unless you can see it confirmed — always direct to the live booking link - Do not promise to "block out" a time manually — the booking link handles this automatically - Do not ask the customer to message you to "check and confirm" — the whole point is they book it themselves instantly RESCHEDULING: If a customer wants to reschedule or cancel, say: "No problem! Use the link in your confirmation email to reschedule or cancel — it only takes a few seconds, and you'll see updated availability instantly."
💡 Why "always send the live link" matters: Your chatbot doesn't have real-time access to your calendar unless connected through advanced integrations. The safest, simplest approach — and the one we recommend for beginners — is letting the chatbot hand off to the live Calendly page, which always shows the real, current availability.

6 Connect, Test, and Go Live

With your calendar set up and your chatbot trained, it's time to put it all together and test it like a real customer would.

1

Add the Booking Content and Retrain

In Chatbase, paste the booking training content from Section 5 as a new text source (or append to your existing one). Click "Retrain" or "Save" — this usually takes under a minute.

2

Update the Instructions

Go to Settings → AI → Instructions. Paste the booking instructions from Section 5 underneath your existing Class 6 instructions. Save.

💡 Keep both sets of instructions: Your chatbot still needs to handle complaints and FAQs from Class 6 — booking is an addition, not a replacement.
3

Test the Full Booking Conversation

Use the live preview in Chatbase. Type these exact messages and check the responses:

→ "I'd like to book an appointment"
→ "Are you free this Saturday?"
→ "What time slots do you have next week?"
→ "I need to cancel my booking"

For each, check: did the chatbot share the correct booking link? Did it avoid guessing specific time slots? Did it sound helpful, not robotic?

⚠️ Click the link yourself during testing. Make sure it opens your actual Calendly page and shows real, correct availability — a broken or wrong link here means every booking attempt fails silently.
4

Make an Actual Test Booking

Go through the entire flow as if you were a customer: ask the chatbot to book, click the link, pick a slot, enter test details, and confirm. Check that the confirmation email arrives and that the slot now shows as unavailable to others.

🎉 If the test booking worked end-to-end — you're done. Your chatbot now handles customer questions, complaints, AND lets people book real appointments — 24 hours a day, without you lifting a finger.

5

Go Live (or Confirm It Already Is)

If your chatbot is already embedded on a website from Class 6, you don't need to re-embed anything — the updates apply automatically. If not, go to Connect → Embed, copy the code, and paste it before </body> on your site.

From now on, every time someone asks "are you free?" — your chatbot answers instantly, and they can book before they even close the chat window.

🚀 Want to go further? If you've connected Make.com before (you may have already used it for Pinterest automation!), you can build a more advanced flow where Chatbase's AI Actions trigger Make.com to create the booking directly inside the chat — without sending the customer to a separate page. This is an optional "pro upgrade" for later once you're comfortable with the basics above.

7 How to Sell This as a Service

Almost every local business that takes appointments is still doing it by message or phone call — and losing bookings to slow replies. This is your opportunity.

What to charge:

  • Add booking to an existing chatbot — Calendly setup + chatbot training: $80–$150
  • Full chatbot + booking system from scratch — customer service + booking + branding: $250–$400
  • Multi-service booking setup — multiple event types (e.g. haircut, coloring, styling) + chatbot: $350–$500

Who to target: Salons and barbershops, dental and beauty clinics, tutors and coaches, consultants, photographers, gyms and personal trainers, home service providers (cleaners, repair technicians). Anywhere a customer currently has to "message to check availability."

How to find clients: Search local business pages and check their bio or website for phrases like "DM to book" or "message us for availability" — that's the exact gap you're filling. Reach out: "I noticed customers have to message you to book — I can set up a system where they book themselves instantly, any time of day. Want a quick demo?"

💡 Pro move: Set up your own Calendly + chatbot demo for a fictional "Sample Salon" or "Sample Clinic." Record a 60-second screen video showing a customer booking an appointment in real time. Send that video — not just a message — to potential clients. Seeing it work is far more convincing than hearing about it.
