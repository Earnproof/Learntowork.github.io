🎓 Series 2 — Build AI Chatbots · Class 6

Build a Free AI Customer Service Chatbot for Your Business

⏱ 30 min read 🛠 Live build-along 📝 Quiz at the end 🆓 Free tools only

1 Your Customers Are Waiting — And Waiting

Someone visits your website at 11 PM. They have a question about your product. They type it. No one replies. They leave. That sale — gone forever. This happens thousands of times every day to businesses that do not have a customer service chatbot.

Without a chatbot: customers wait hours and leave. With an AI chatbot: instant answer, happy customer, more sales.

In Class 4, you built a basic chatbot for a website. That was a good starting point. Class 6 is different. Today you build a customer service chatbot — one that handles real complaints, difficult questions, refund requests, and angry customers — professionally, every single time, without you being present.

This is the chatbot that businesses actually pay freelancers to build. After this class, you can charge $150–$500 to set one up for any small business.

⚡ What you need: A free Chatbase account (you already have one from Class 4!). A computer or phone. 30 minutes. That is all.

2 Customer Service Chatbot vs Basic Chatbot — What Is the Difference?

A basic chatbot answers simple questions. A customer service chatbot does much more — it handles difficult situations intelligently.

Basic chatbot = answers questions. Customer service chatbot = handles situations — with empathy, escalation, and resolution.

FeatureBasic Chatbot (Class 4)Customer Service Chatbot (Class 6)
Answers FAQs✅ Yes✅ Yes
Handles complaints❌ No✅ Yes — with empathy scripts
Processes refund requests❌ No✅ Yes — collects info, guides process
Handles angry customers❌ No — gives generic reply✅ Yes — de-escalation responses
Escalates to human❌ No✅ Yes — knows when to hand over
Tracks order status❌ No✅ Yes — guides customer to check
Collects customer details❌ No✅ Yes — name, order number, issue
🍋 Think of it like this

A basic chatbot is like a notice board — it shows information. A customer service chatbot is like a trained support agent — it listens, empathises, solves problems, and knows when to call the manager. Same tool, completely different training.

3 What Your Chatbot Will Handle

Before you build, you need to know what situations your chatbot will face. Here are the 5 most common customer service scenarios — and how your chatbot will deal with each one:

📋 Scenario 1

Simple FAQ

Customer asks: "What are your delivery times?" → Chatbot answers instantly from training data.

😤 Scenario 2

Complaint

Customer says: "My order arrived damaged." → Chatbot apologises, collects order number, explains next steps.

💰 Scenario 3

Refund Request

Customer asks: "I want a refund." → Chatbot explains refund policy, collects details, tells them what happens next.

😡 Scenario 4

Angry Customer

Customer types in caps: "THIS IS UNACCEPTABLE!!!" → Chatbot stays calm, validates feelings, offers solution.

🔁 Scenario 5

Escalation

Issue is too complex → Chatbot says: "Let me connect you with our team" and collects contact details.

4 Prepare Your Training Content

The most important part of a customer service chatbot is not the tool — it is the content you give it. Garbage in, garbage out. Good training = professional chatbot.

Open a text document and write the following. Be as detailed and specific as possible — every detail you add means one less question your chatbot cannot answer.

📝 Customer Service Training Content TemplateBUSINESS INFORMATION Business Name: [Your Business Name] What we sell: [Describe your product or service in 2-3 sentences] Website: [Your website URL] Contact email: [your@email.com] Contact WhatsApp: [your number] Business hours: [e.g. Monday–Friday, 9 AM – 6 PM] PRODUCTS AND PRICES [List your main products/services with prices] Example: - Logo Design Package: $50 (includes 3 concepts + 2 revisions) - Social Media Kit: $80 (10 posts + cover images) - Full Brand Package: $150 (logo + social kit + business card) ORDERING PROCESS How to order: [Explain your exact process step by step] Example: Customer sends message on WhatsApp → We discuss requirements → Customer pays 50% → Work begins → Delivered in 5 days → Final 50% paid → Files sent DELIVERY AND TIMELINES Standard delivery: [X] business days Rush delivery: [X] days for extra [amount] How files are delivered: [Google Drive / Email / WhatsApp] REFUND AND REVISION POLICY Revisions included: [How many — e.g. 2 free revisions per order] Refund policy: [e.g. Full refund if work not started. 50% refund if work in progress. No refund after final delivery.] How to request: [Email / WhatsApp] FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS Q: Do you work with international clients? A: Yes, we work with clients worldwide. Payment via PayPal or Wise. Q: Can I see samples of your work? A: Yes! Visit [your portfolio link] or ask us to send samples on WhatsApp. Q: What file formats do you deliver? A: PNG, JPG, PDF, and editable source files (AI/PSD) on request. Q: How do I pay? A: PayPal, bank transfer, or Easypaisa/JazzCash for Pakistani clients. Q: What if I'm not happy with the work? A: We offer 2 free revisions. If still not satisfied, we discuss a solution — we want you happy with the result. COMPLAINT HANDLING If a customer complains about quality: Apologise sincerely, ask for details, offer a revision or solution. If an order is delayed: Apologise, give a new timeline, offer a small discount on next order. If a product arrived damaged: Apologise, ask for a photo, arrange replacement or refund. ESCALATION TRIGGERS Escalate to human team if: - Customer asks to speak to a manager - Refund request is over $100 - Customer is still angry after 2 chatbot responses - Issue involves legal or fraud concerns

⚠️ Fill this in for YOUR business. Do not leave the template text — replace every placeholder with real information. If you are building this for a client, ask them to fill in their details first before you train the chatbot.

5 The Instructions — This Is What Makes It a Customer Service Bot

The instructions you write inside Chatbase tell your chatbot how to behave — not just what to know. This is where a basic chatbot becomes a customer service professional.

Copy this template, read it carefully, and edit it for your specific business before pasting into Chatbase:

📋 Customer Service Instructions Template — Paste into ChatbaseYou are a friendly, professional customer service assistant for [BUSINESS NAME]. YOUR PERSONALITY: - Always polite, calm and helpful — even if the customer is rude - Use the customer's name if they give it - Keep replies short — 2 to 4 sentences maximum - Never argue with a customer. Never say "you are wrong." - If you do not know something, say: "I want to make sure I give you the right answer — please contact us at [EMAIL] for this." HANDLING COMPLAINTS: When a customer complains, always: 1. Apologise first — genuinely. Example: "I'm really sorry to hear that happened." 2. Acknowledge their frustration. Example: "I completely understand how frustrating this must be." 3. Ask for the details you need. Example: "Could you share your order number so I can help you better?" 4. Explain what happens next. Example: "Once I have your order number, our team will review and get back to you within 24 hours." HANDLING ANGRY CUSTOMERS (ALL CAPS, STRONG LANGUAGE): - Never match their energy. Stay calm. - Validate their feelings first: "I hear you, and I completely understand your frustration." - Offer a concrete next step immediately — do not just apologise and leave it open. - If they remain very upset after 2 responses, say: "I want to make sure this is fully resolved for you — let me connect you with our team directly. Please share your name and email and someone will contact you within 2 hours." REFUND REQUESTS: - Explain the refund policy clearly and calmly - Do not promise refunds you are not sure about — say "let me check this for you" - Collect: customer name, order number, reason for refund request - Tell them: "Our team will review your request and respond within 24 hours" ESCALATION — WHEN TO HAND OVER TO A HUMAN: Say: "I want to make sure you get the best help possible. Let me connect you with our team directly. Could you share your name and email address? Someone will get back to you within [X hours]." Escalate when: - Customer asks for a manager or human - Issue involves a refund or dispute - Customer is still upset after 2 responses - You genuinely do not have the answer THINGS YOU MUST NEVER DO: - Never make up information - Never promise something the business cannot deliver - Never be rude or dismissive - Never discuss competitor businesses - Never share private customer information
💡 Why the instructions matter more than the training data: The training data tells your chatbot what to say. The instructions tell it how to say it. A chatbot with great training data but no instructions will be robotic and unhelpful. A chatbot with both will feel like a real professional.

6 Build Your Customer Service Chatbot — Step by Step

You already know Chatbase from Class 4. This build is faster because you know the platform. The difference is entirely in the content and instructions.

1

Create a New Chatbot in Chatbase

Log in to chatbase.co. Click "New Chatbot". Choose "Text" as your data source. You will see the text input box — this is where your training content goes.

💡 Tip: Give your chatbot a professional name like "Support Assistant" or "[Business Name] Help" — not just "Bot." The name shows in the chat window and affects first impressions.
2

Paste Your Training Content

Copy everything from the training content template you filled in Section 4. Paste it into the text box in Chatbase. The more detailed and accurate your content is, the better your chatbot will perform.

Click "Create Chatbot". Training takes about 30 seconds. You will be taken to your chatbot's dashboard when it is ready.

💡 Multiple sources: You can also add your website URL as a second data source — Chatbase will read all the text on your site. Do this after pasting your text to give the chatbot even more information to work with.
3

Add the Customer Service Instructions

Go to Settings → AI → Instructions (or "System Prompt" depending on your Chatbase version). Delete any default text. Paste your full customer service instructions from Section 5.

Scroll down and set the Model to GPT-4o mini if available — it gives better, more natural customer service responses than GPT-3.5 and uses less credit than GPT-4.

⚠️ Do not skip the instructions. Without them, your chatbot will answer questions but will not know how to handle complaints, angry customers, or when to escalate. The instructions are what make this a customer service tool — not just a FAQ bot.
4

Set Up the Welcome Message and Appearance

Go to the Appearance tab. Set:

→ Chatbot Name: "[Business Name] Support"
→ Welcome Message: "Hi! 👋 I'm here to help with any questions, orders, or issues. What can I help you with today?"
→ Placeholder text: "Type your question here..."
→ Color: Match your brand color

💡 Welcome message matters: A specific, warm welcome message like "Hi! Ask me about orders, refunds, or anything else 😊" gets 3x more engagement than a generic "Hello. How can I help?"
5

Test With Real Customer Scenarios

This is the most important step. Use the live preview on the right side of Chatbase to test your chatbot with difficult scenarios — not just easy questions. Test these exact messages:

→ "MY ORDER NEVER ARRIVED AND THIS IS RIDICULOUS!!!"
→ "I want a full refund right now"
→ "Your product is complete garbage"
→ "Let me speak to your manager"
→ "How much does the logo package cost?"
→ "Do you work with clients in the UK?"

For each response, ask yourself: Would this response make a real customer feel heard and helped? If not, improve your training content or instructions and retrain.

⚠️ Never go live without testing angry customer scenarios. These are exactly the situations where a bad chatbot response will make things worse — and a good one will save the relationship.
6

Embed on Your Website and Go Live

Go to Connect → Embed. Copy the embed code. Paste it just before the </body> tag in your website's HTML. Save and upload. Your customer service chatbot is now live — handling inquiries 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

🎉 Your AI customer service chatbot is live. It handles FAQs, complaints, refund requests, and angry customers — professionally, every time, without you doing anything. Customers get instant responses at 3 AM. You sleep. Everyone wins.

7 How to Sell This as a Service

Every small business with a website needs this — but most have no idea how to build it. That gap is your opportunity.

What to charge:

  • Basic customer service chatbot — FAQ + complaint handling: $100–$150
  • Full customer service chatbot — FAQ + complaints + escalation + custom branding: $200–$350
  • Premium with monthly maintenance — build + update training + monitor monthly: $300 build + $50/month

Who to target: Online stores, restaurants with delivery, freelancers with clients, tutoring centres, salons and beauty services, real estate agents, coaching businesses. Any business that gets repetitive customer questions is a potential client.

How to find clients: Look at local business Instagram pages. Check if they have a website. Check if they have a chatbot — most do not. Send them a DM: "I noticed your website does not have a chatbot — I can build you one that answers customer questions automatically. Want me to show you a demo?"

💡 Pro move: Build a demo chatbot for a specific niche — like a restaurant chatbot or a clothing store chatbot — before you approach clients. When they see a working demo relevant to their business, conversion rate is much higher than just describing what you can do.
