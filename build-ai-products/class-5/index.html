3 Five Words You Must Know Before You Build

Make.com has its own vocabulary. You will see these words everywhere inside the platform. Learn them now so nothing confuses you when you start clicking.

These five terms are everything you need to understand Make.com before you start building your first scenario.

1. Scenario

A Scenario is your complete automation workflow — the whole thing from start to finish. Think of it as a recipe. It has a beginning (what triggers it), a middle (what happens step by step), and an end (the final output). Everything you build on Make.com is called a Scenario.

2. Module

A Module is one single step inside a scenario. "Search for an email" is one module. "Send a message to Slack" is another module. "Ask ChatGPT a question" is another module. You connect modules together like train carriages — each one receives data from the previous, does something, and passes the result forward.

3. Trigger

The Trigger is the very first module — the event that starts everything. Nothing runs until the trigger fires. A trigger could be: a new email arrives, a form is submitted, a new row is added to a spreadsheet, a scheduled time, or a file being uploaded. Without a trigger, your scenario just sits there doing nothing.

4. Operation

Every time one module runs once, that counts as 1 Operation. The free plan gives you 1,000 operations per month. If your scenario has 3 modules and it runs 10 times, that is 30 operations used. Operations reset every month. For most beginners, 1,000 operations is more than enough to build and test multiple real automations.

5. Data Bundle

A Data Bundle is the package of information that flows from one module to the next. For example, when a form is submitted, the bundle contains the person's name, email, and message. Each module receives this bundle, does something with it, and passes an updated bundle forward. You can use any piece of bundle data in any later module — just click on it.

💡 One more: Filters. A Filter sits between two modules and says: "Only continue if this condition is true." For example: only process an email if the sender is a client, or only send an alert if the order value is over $50. Filters give you precise control so your automation does not run for every single event — only the ones that matter.

6 Build Your First Scenario — Step by Step

We are going to build a real, working automation together: When a Google Form is submitted → Make sends the response to ChatGPT → ChatGPT writes a personalised thank-you reply → Make saves it to Google Sheets. Follow every step exactly. Read each one fully before you click.

1

Log In and Create a New Scenario

Once inside Make.com, look for the blue "Create a new scenario" button on your dashboard. Click it. You will be taken to a blank white canvas — this is where you build your workflow visually.

In the centre of the canvas you will see a large circle with a "+" icon. This is where your first module goes. Think of it as the empty first page of your recipe. Nothing runs until you fill it.

💡 Get comfortable with the canvas first: You can zoom in and out using your scroll wheel. You can move around by clicking and dragging the empty background. Spend one minute just exploring before adding anything — it will make the rest much less confusing.
2

Add Your Trigger: Google Forms — Watch Responses

Click the large "+" circle. A search panel appears. Type "Google Forms" and click the app when it shows. From the module list, choose "Watch Responses".

Make will ask you to connect your Google account. Click "Add", sign in, and grant permission. This is a one-time setup — Make remembers this connection for all future scenarios you build.

After connecting, select the specific Google Form you want to monitor. If you do not have one yet, open Google Forms in another tab, create a simple form with a "Name" and "Message" field, save it, then come back and select it here.

💡 What "Watch Responses" means: Make will check your Google Form for new submissions at regular intervals. Each new submission fires the trigger and your scenario runs. On the free plan, the minimum check interval is 15 minutes — meaning automation runs within 15 minutes of each submission.
3

Add the OpenAI Module: Create a Completion

Hover over the Google Forms module on your canvas. A small "+" appears on its right side. Click it to add the next module. Search for "OpenAI" and select it. Choose "Create a Completion".

Make will ask for your OpenAI API key. This is NOT your ChatGPT password — it is a separate key from platform.openai.com/api-keys. Create a free account there, generate a key, and paste it into Make. New accounts come with free credits — enough to test this scenario many times over.

Now write your prompt. Click inside the "Prompt" field. You will see small coloured tags — these are data bundles from your Google Form. Click the bundle tags to insert the actual form data into your prompt dynamically. For example:

📝 Example Prompt — Edit This for Your Own UseWrite a short, warm, and professional thank-you reply to this message. The person's name is: [click the Name bundle tag here] Their message was: [click the Message bundle tag here] Keep your reply to 3 sentences maximum. Be genuine and specific to what they wrote. End with: "We will get back to you within 24 hours."
💡 Which model to choose: In the "Model" dropdown, select gpt-3.5-turbo. It is fast and uses minimal API credits. Only switch to GPT-4 if you need much higher quality for professional client-facing use — it costs more credits per run.
4

Add Google Sheets: Save the AI Reply

Add another module after OpenAI. Search "Google Sheets" and choose "Add a Row". Connect your Google account. Select a spreadsheet — or create a new Google Sheet with these columns: Name, Original Message, AI Reply, Date.

Now map the data bundles to each column:

→ Name column → click the "Name" bundle from your Google Form
→ Original Message column → click the "Message" bundle from your Google Form
→ AI Reply column → click the "choices[] → message → content" bundle from the OpenAI module — this is the actual ChatGPT text
→ Date column → type {{now}} to auto-insert the current date and time

⚠️ Finding the right OpenAI bundle: Look for a field called "choices[] → message → content" or similar — this is ChatGPT's actual reply. If you accidentally select a different bundle field, your sheet will fill with numbers or empty values. If this happens, just click the field, clear it, and choose a different bundle option.
5

Run Once to Test Everything

Before you activate, you must test. Click "Run once" at the bottom left of your canvas. This tells Make: run the scenario one time right now and show me exactly what happens at each step.

Now go to your Google Form in another tab and submit a test response — use a real name and write a short message. Come back to Make. You should see coloured bubbles appearing on each module: green = success, red = error.

Click each green bubble to inspect exactly what data flowed through. For the OpenAI module, you should see the full ChatGPT reply that was generated. For Google Sheets, you should see a confirmation that a row was added. Open your spreadsheet — one new row should now exist with all the data correctly filled in.

💡 If you get an error: Click the red bubble to read the error message. The most common causes are: wrong API key, spreadsheet column names do not match what Make expects, or the form has no responses yet. Fix the issue and click "Run once" again. Errors during testing are completely normal — they are how you learn how Make.com works.
6

Activate Your Scenario

Once your test run works perfectly with no errors, it is time to go live. At the bottom left of the canvas, you will see a toggle switch showing "OFF". Click it to turn it "ON". A confirmation will appear — click confirm.

Your scenario is now active and running in the background. From this moment forward, Make.com will automatically check your Google Form every 15 minutes. Any new submission will trigger the full chain — ChatGPT generates a reply, the reply gets saved to your spreadsheet — with zero input from you.

🎉 Your first AI automation is live. Every time someone fills your form, AI will automatically process their message and save a personalised reply — while you sleep, while you are busy, while you do anything else entirely. You built this with no code, in under 30 minutes, using only free tools.

💡 Want to also send the reply by email? Add one more module after Google Sheets: search "Gmail" → choose "Send an Email" → map the recipient's email from the form bundle, and the ChatGPT reply as the email body. Now every person who fills your form gets a personalised AI-written reply in their inbox within 15 minutes — automatically.
