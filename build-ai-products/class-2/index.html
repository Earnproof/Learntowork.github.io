2 ChatGPT vs Claude vs Gemini: Which One Wins?

People ask me every week: "Which AI should I use?" The answer is not one — it is knowing when to use which one. Here is the comparison nobody else gives you honestly.

Tool Best For Weakness Free Plan?
ChatGPT (OpenAI)Most Popular General tasks, coding help, image generation (DALL·E), huge plugin ecosystem Can hallucinate facts confidently; free plan uses older GPT-4o mini ✅ Yes (limited)
Claude (Anthropic) Long documents, nuanced writing, following complex instructions precisely No image generation; smaller plugin ecosystem ✅ Yes (limited)
Gemini (Google) Real-time web search, Google Workspace integration, research tasks Less consistent on creative tasks; personality feels robotic ✅ Yes
Copilot (Microsoft) Microsoft Office integration, Windows tasks, free GPT-4 access Less flexible outside Microsoft ecosystem ✅ Yes (GPT-4 free)

My personal rule: I use ChatGPT for most tasks because the ecosystem is largest and GPT-4o is genuinely powerful. I switch to Claude when I have a long document to analyze or need very precise writing. I use Gemini when I need something researched from today's news.

For this course, we focus on ChatGPT because it is what most people have access to and it teaches skills that transfer to every other tool. The techniques you learn here work on Claude, Gemini, and every AI that comes after.

⚠️ Important: Every AI tool — including ChatGPT — can confidently state wrong information. This is called "hallucination." Always verify facts, statistics, and anything medical, legal, or financial before acting on AI output. AI is a starting point, not a final source.

3 The ChatGPT Interface Decoded

Most people click "New Chat" and start typing. They are missing features that would save them hours. Let me walk you through what everything actually does.

Every feature labeled. Most people use only the text box — you will use all of it.

Feature What It Does & Why It Matters
Custom Instructions Tell ChatGPT who you are and how you want it to respond — permanently. Set this once and every conversation automatically knows your background, preferences, and style. Game changer for consistent output.
Memory ChatGPT remembers facts about you across conversations. Enable it in Settings → Personalization. You can view and delete what it remembers anytime.
GPT Store / GPTs Pre-built AI assistants for specific tasks: resume writing, SEO, language learning, coding. You will build your own Custom GPT in Class 3.
Model Selector GPT-4o for complex tasks. GPT-4o mini for quick, simple questions (faster). o1 for deep reasoning and math problems.
File Upload Upload PDFs, spreadsheets, images, code files. ChatGPT reads and analyzes them. Great for summarizing documents or analyzing data.
Voice Mode Have a real conversation with GPT-4o using voice. Useful for practicing languages, thinking out loud, or hands-free work.
DALL·E Image Generation Generate images directly in chat. Type what you want to see. Covered in detail in Series 2.

Action step right now: Open ChatGPT, go to your profile icon → Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions. Fill in both boxes. Tell it your profession, your goals, how long you want responses, and your preferred tone. This single step will improve every ChatGPT response you get from this moment forward.

🔧 Sample Custom Instruction setup:
About me: "I am a freelance social media manager working with small businesses. I am intermediate level, not a beginner. I work mainly in English."

How I want responses: "Be direct. No filler phrases like 'Great question!' Give bullet points for lists. Keep responses under 300 words unless I ask for more. Always give me practical, actionable advice."

4 5 Techniques That Separate Pros from Beginners

These five techniques took me from getting mediocre AI output to getting results I can actually use and sell. They are not tricks — they are fundamental ways of communicating with AI that most tutorials never explain.

1

Chain Prompting — Break It Down

Do not ask for everything at once. Break complex tasks into a sequence of smaller prompts. Each response becomes the input for the next. You get more control, better quality, and fewer errors.

❌ Basic: "Write me a complete marketing plan for my bakery."

✅ Chained:
Prompt 1: "What are the top 3 marketing channels for a local bakery in 2026? List them with one sentence each."
Prompt 2: "Now for each channel you listed, give me one specific, low-cost action I can take this week."
Prompt 3: "Turn these actions into a 4-week calendar with daily tasks."
2

Persona Stacking — Make AI an Expert

From Class 1, you know the Role component of the formula. Persona stacking goes deeper — you give ChatGPT multiple layers of expertise and perspective, not just a job title.

❌ Basic role: "You are a marketing expert."

✅ Stacked persona: "You are a marketing strategist with 15 years of experience growing small businesses on budgets under $500/month. You are known for being brutally honest about what does not work and practical about what does. You speak plainly, never use jargon, and always give specific examples."
3

Iterative Refinement — Treat It Like a Draft

The first response is almost never the best response. The pros treat every ChatGPT output as a first draft and iterate. Use follow-up commands to improve specific parts without starting over.

Powerful follow-up commands:
→ "Make this 50% shorter without losing the key points."
→ "The third paragraph is too formal. Rewrite it more conversationally."
→ "Give me 3 alternative versions of the headline only."
→ "What is the weakest part of this and how would you improve it?"
→ "Add a story or real-world example to make point 2 more relatable."
4

Template Creation — Work Once, Use Forever

Stop writing prompts from scratch every time. Build reusable prompt templates with [PLACEHOLDERS] for the parts that change. Save them in a document and fill in the blanks each time. This is how professionals 10x their speed.

✅ Reusable template example:
"You are a [ROLE] with deep expertise in [FIELD]. Write a [FORMAT] about [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. The tone should be [TONE]. Include [SPECIFIC ELEMENT]. Keep it under [WORD COUNT] words. End with [CALL TO ACTION]."

Fill in once → save → reuse for any task.
5

Conversation Threading — Use Context

ChatGPT remembers everything in your current conversation. This means you can build on previous answers, refer back to earlier content, and create a flowing workflow — all without repeating yourself.

✅ Threading in action:
"Based on the marketing plan you just created, now write three Instagram captions for the first week. Match the brand voice from the plan."

"Using the bakery persona we established, now write a response to a 1-star review about slow service."

The AI already has all the context. You just keep building.

Here is what makes these five techniques powerful together: they are a system. You stack a persona → chain your prompts → refine the output → save it as a template → thread the next task onto it. In 30 minutes, you can produce what would take a junior team member a full day.

5 Your Build: Create a Reusable AI Workflow

Theory is good. A workflow you actually use is better. Right now, you are going to build a personal prompt template for the task you do most — whether that is writing, studying, client work, or anything else.

🛠 Build Your Personal AI Workflow Template

Follow these 4 steps. By the end you will have something you can use today and every day after.

1️⃣
Pick your most repeated task Writing social media captions? Summarizing long articles? Replying to emails? Studying for exams? Writing code comments? Pick the one task you do most often.
2️⃣
Build the stacked persona Who is the ideal expert for this task? What experience do they have? What is their style? What do they always include and what do they avoid?
3️⃣
Write the template with placeholders Use [BRACKETS] for the parts that change each time. Keep the instruction parts fixed.
4️⃣
Test it right now Open ChatGPT, paste your template, fill in the brackets, and run it. Refine based on the output.

Example completed workflow template:

You are a social media strategist who specializes in helping small businesses grow on Instagram without paid ads. You write in a friendly, direct tone — never corporate. You always include a hook in the first line, value in the middle, and a clear call to action at the end.

Write 3 Instagram captions for [BUSINESS TYPE] about [TOPIC/PRODUCT].

The target audience is [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]. The brand voice is [TONE: e.g. warm and funny / professional and trustworthy]. Recent context: [ANY RELEVANT INFO like a sale, event, or season].

Format each caption as: Hook (1 line) → Value (2-3 lines) → CTA (1 line) → 5 relevant hashtags. Number each caption 1, 2, 3.

One more thing before we move on. The most common mistake I see is people writing a great template, using it once, then forgetting it. Save your template somewhere you will actually find it — a Google Doc called "My AI Prompts," a note on your phone, a bookmark folder. The value compounds every time you use it.
