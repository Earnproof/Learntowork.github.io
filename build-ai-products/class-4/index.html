2 What Is a Website Chatbot — Simply Explained

Before we build, let us make sure we understand exactly what we are building. No technical words. Just a clear picture.

📖 What Is a Website Chatbot?

A website chatbot is a small chat window that appears on a webpage. When a visitor types a question, the chatbot reads it, searches through the information you gave it during setup, and writes back an answer. It happens in seconds, automatically, without you doing anything.

🍋 Think of it like this

You write a book about your business — your services, prices, FAQs, hours. Now imagine a smart librarian who has read that book from cover to cover and can answer any question about it instantly. The chatbot IS that librarian. You write the book once. The chatbot answers questions forever.

Here is the key difference from everything we have built before:

What We BuiltWhere It LivesWho Can Use It
Custom GPT (Class 3) Inside ChatGPT app Only people with a ChatGPT account
Website Chatbot (This class) On any website Anyone in the world — no account needed

This is why website chatbots are so powerful for business. Your customers, your readers, your visitors — they do not need to download anything or create an account. They just visit your page and the chatbot is already there, waiting.

4 Build Your Chatbot — Step by Step

Follow every step exactly. Read each one completely before you do it. By the end of this section, you will have a real working chatbot with an embed code ready to paste into any website.

1

Create a New Chatbot

Once you are logged into Chatbase, you will see your dashboard. Click the "New Chatbot" button. You will be taken to a page that asks: how do you want to train your chatbot?

You will see three options. Think of these as three ways to teach your chatbot what to know:

→ Website — paste your website URL and Chatbase reads all the text on your site automatically
→ Files — upload PDFs, Word documents, or text files
→ Text — type or paste information directly

💡 Best choice for beginners: Click "Text" and type some information about yourself or an imaginary business. This is the fastest way to see your chatbot working. You can add more data sources later.
2

Add Your Training Data

This is where you teach your chatbot what to know. Whatever you put here is what your chatbot will be able to answer questions about.

If you chose Text, type information like this example:

📝 Example Training Text — Copy & Edit Business Name: Layla's Freelance Design Studio Services: - Logo design: Starting from $50 - Social media graphics: $30 per set of 10 - Website banners: $40 per set Turnaround time: 3-5 business days for all orders. Payment methods: PayPal, bank transfer, Payoneer. How to order: Send a message on Instagram @layladesigns or email hello@layladesigns.com Frequently asked questions: Q: Do you offer revisions? A: Yes, 2 free revisions are included with every order. Q: Can I see samples of your work? A: Yes, visit instagram.com/layladesigns for my portfolio. Q: Do you work with international clients? A: Yes, I work with clients from all countries.

Replace this with your own information — your name, your services, your business, or whatever topic you want your chatbot to help with. The more detail you add, the better your chatbot's answers will be.

💡 Important rule: Only add information that is true and accurate. Your chatbot will answer based exactly on what you write here. If you write wrong prices, your chatbot will tell visitors the wrong price.
3

Create the Chatbot and Set Instructions

After adding your data, click "Create Chatbot". Chatbase will read your information and train the AI. This takes about 30 seconds.

Now you will be in your chatbot's settings. Find the "Instructions" or "System Prompt" section. This is where you give your chatbot its personality and rules — exactly like the Custom GPT instructions from Class 3.

Here is a simple instruction template:

📋 Instructions Template You are a friendly assistant for [YOUR NAME / BUSINESS NAME]. Your job is to answer questions about [YOUR TOPIC] based only on the information you have been given. Always be polite and helpful. If someone asks something you do not know, say: "I do not have that information right now. Please contact us at [YOUR EMAIL] for more help." Do not make up answers. Only use the information provided to you. Keep your answers short and clear — 2 to 4 sentences maximum.
💡 Why "do not make up answers" matters: Without this instruction, AI sometimes invents information that sounds correct but is not. This rule tells your chatbot to stay honest and only say what it actually knows.
4

Customize How It Looks

Go to the "Appearance" tab in your chatbot settings. Here you can make your chatbot look professional:

→ Chatbot Name — give it a name like "Layla's Assistant" or "Help Bot"
→ Welcome Message — the first thing it says when someone opens the chat. Example: "Hi! 👋 I'm Layla's assistant. How can I help you today?"
→ Color — match it to your brand colors. Purple, blue, green — whatever fits
→ Chat Icon — you can upload a small logo or leave the default

💡 Welcome message matters a lot: Most visitors will decide in 3 seconds if they want to chat. A warm, specific welcome message ("Hi! Ask me anything about our design services 🎨") gets far more engagement than a generic "Hello."
5

Test It Before You Share

On the right side of the Chatbase dashboard, you will see a live preview of your chatbot. This is your chance to test it before anyone else sees it.

Test it like a real visitor would — not like someone who built it. Ask it questions your visitors might actually ask:

→ "What are your prices?"
→ "How do I place an order?"
→ "Do you work with international clients?"
→ "What happens if I'm not happy with the work?"

If any answer is wrong, go back to your training text and fix the information. Then click "Retrain" and test again.

⚠️ Do not skip testing! A chatbot that gives wrong answers is worse than no chatbot. It breaks trust instantly. Test at least 5-10 questions before you share your chatbot with anyone.
6

Get Your Embed Code and Add It to a Website

This is the final step. Go to the "Connect" or "Embed" tab in your chatbot settings. You will see something like this:

💻 Your Embed Code Will Look Like This<script src="https://www.chatbase.co/embed.min.js" chatbotId="YOUR-CHATBOT-ID" domain="www.chatbase.co" defer> </script>

What to do with this code: Copy the entire code. Open your website's HTML file. Find the line that says </body> (near the very end of the file). Paste the code on the line just before that closing tag. Save the file. Open your website in a browser. You will see a small chat bubble appear in the bottom right corner. Click it. Your chatbot is live.

🎉 Your chatbot is now live on your website! Anyone who visits that page can now chat with your AI. You did not write a single line of code. You trained it with your own information. It is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — even while you sleep.

💡 No website yet? No problem. You can share the Chatbase public link directly — it gives you a URL like chatbase.co/chatbot/your-id that anyone can visit. Use this for testing or share on WhatsApp, Instagram, or anywhere you want.

5 How People Use This to Earn Money

You just learned a skill that businesses pay real money for. Here is exactly how.

Way 1 — Sell it as a service

Many small businesses — restaurants, salons, freelancers, online shops — want a chatbot on their website but have no idea how to build one. You can offer this as a service on Fiverr or Upwork for $50–$200 per chatbot. It takes you 30 minutes to build. That is $100+/hour equivalent.

Way 2 — Use it for your own business

If you offer any service — freelance writing, design, tutoring, selling products — add a chatbot to your portfolio or website. It answers client questions while you sleep. Clients feel heard and get faster responses, which means more bookings for you.

Way 3 — Build niche chatbots

Build a specialized chatbot for a specific topic — cooking recipes, Quran recitation help, university application guidance for Pakistani students, job interview practice — and share the public link. Grow an audience around it. Monetize later through partnerships or premium versions.

💡 Real example: A student in Lahore built a chatbot trained on a university's admission FAQs. She shared the link in Facebook groups for students applying to that university. Within a week, hundreds of students were using it. She got freelance inquiries from other universities asking her to build theirs too.
